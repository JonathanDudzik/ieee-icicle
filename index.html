Understanding the Learning Technologies Standards Landscape

The learning technology standards is complex and ever changing, work with Industry experts to understand and build together a map of landscape. This will be a hands-on session divided into three activities. In the first activity, participants organized in groups will have the opportunity to play with categorizations for learning engineering data standards and to map examples of standards and communities proposing standards to categories. The second activity will be focused on uses of standards, with a focus on the needs they cover and their evolution. Some examples will be provided and groups will enrich their maps. Finally, groups will use the maps to identify gaps for the potential development of new standards and will discuss barriers that may hinder the adoption of existing standards. Maps and conclusions achieved in the groups will be shared to the whole-workshop participants after each activity.

Founders Hall 121

What is the Role of the Learner in an AI-augmented Learning Process?

We are beginning to realize the benefits that AI will bring to increasing productivity in nearly all market segments. The ability of AI to take responsibility for tasks at which humans are less efficient allowing them to focus on those that require human intervention is at the heart of increased productivity. Education is unique in that the product is growth the the cognitive abilities of the human where the role of AI should inform but not supplant the learning process. In education, the learner needs not only engage with the domain specific content but also develop their cognitive skills and the reflection on those skills (metacognition). These are areas in which AI would traditionally be applied to streamline the process yet, in this case, the associated inefficiencies lie at the heart of the learning process. This panel will explore where and when learning engineers draw the line between AI and engaging the learner as owner of their learning.

Founders Hall 113

Interoperability and Analytics

Intelligent learning and working environment in Industry 4.0 age

The need and attention to interoperability among devices and transfer of data between systems is more pronounced than ever before. This session will address the importance of interoperability across multiple contexts including educational innovation, research and corporate settings as well as primarily state-of-the-art perspectives from those working on standards to ensure interoperability in learning systems. Enhancing the user experience and combating silos of information integrating data from different systems is the holy grail for learning engineers. Presenters will address how interoperability standards fit into the overall landscape of education standards along with addressing what the role and capacity an informed solution provider could play when innovating in these spaces.

xAPI, Credentialing and Blockchain for Learning Ecosystems

Talent development is a crucial support for business outcomes, but it’s always hard to measure ROI of L&D in terms of business outcomes. What if we can let employees being CEO of their jobs and learning as micro-enterprises and share the business impact of their works directly? xAPI and Blockchain can keep/exchange evidence of value creations and credentials, execute smart contracts according to predefined business agreements, and make disparate learning and work systems work together. The big picture from Blockchain with xAPI data can let us model managerial strategy as well as individual’s behaviors to continuously improve performance of organizations, teams, and individuals, furthermore, individuals can keep life-long evidence of their competencies. This session is strongly recommended for conference attendees interested to join/co-lead a new standard/ecosystem initiative.
